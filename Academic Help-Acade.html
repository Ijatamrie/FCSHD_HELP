Bachelor of Cognitive Sciences (CS)

- Cognitive Psychology: Study of mental processes such as perception, memory, and problem-solving.

- Cognitive Neuroscience: Understanding the neural mechanisms underlying cognitive functions.

- Human-Computer Interaction: Designing and evaluating user interfaces and interactions.

- Artificial Intelligence: Introduction to AI concepts, algorithms, and applications.

- Learning Sciences: Examining how people learn and how to create effective learning environments.



Bachelor of Human Resource Development (HRD)

- Human Resource Management: Principles of managing an organization's workforce.

- Training and Development: Methods and techniques for developing employee skills.

- Organizational Behavior: Study of individual and group behavior in organizational settings.

- Talent Management: Strategies for attracting, developing, and retaining talent.

- Labor Relations: Understanding the relationship between management and employees.



Bachelor of Psychology (PSY)

- Developmental Psychology: Study of human growth and development across the lifespan.

- Clinical Psychology: Assessment and treatment of mental health disorders.

- Social Psychology: Examination of how social influences affect individual behavior.

- Cognitive Psychology: Study of internal mental processes.

- Research Methods: Techniques and methodologies for conducting psychological research.



Bachelor of Counseling (Couns)

- Counseling Theories: Overview of different approaches and theories in counseling.

- Therapeutic Techniques: Practical skills for conducting effective therapy sessions.

- Ethics in Counseling: Ethical considerations and professional standards in counseling practice.

- Group Counseling: Techniques and dynamics of counseling in group settings.

- Career Counseling: Helping clients with career development and decision-making.
